 Sri Lankan Turtle Population

There are 250 species of turtles on Earth, seven of which are marine turtles. The other 243 species are terrestrial turtles, also known as tortoises, which live in freshwater ponds and marshes. The Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas), Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata), Olive Ridley (Lepidochelys olivacea), Loggerhead (Caretta caretta), and Leatherback (Dermochelys coriacea) are five of the seven species of sea turtles that nest on Sri Lankan beaches. In addition, Sri Lanka is index to one terrestrial tortoise, the Star tortoise (Geochelone elegans), and three freshwater turtle species: Parker's Black Turtle (Melanochelys trijuga pakeri), Sri Lanka Black Turtle (Melanochelys trijuga thermalis), and the Soft or Flapshell Turtle (Lissemys punctata punctata).

Why Turtles Are Important ?

For more than 100 million years, sea turtles have been essential to preserving the health of the waters across the world. These functions include everything from preserving healthy coral reef ecosystems to moving vital nutrients from the oceans to beaches and coastal dunes.

Because sea turtles have been nearly eradicated from many regions of the world, significant changes have taken place in the waters. Sea turtles are facing extinction due to pressures brought on by humans, including commercial fishing, the loss of nesting habitat, and climate change. Sea turtle populations are declining, and with them, their capacity to perform essential tasks for ocean ecosystems.

How Long Do Turtles Live ?

Turtles are widely known for having longer lifespans than many other reptiles, which is no mystery. Some turtle species can live for 100 years or longer. Although various factors might affect a turtle's lifespan, several common kinds of aquatic turtles can live into their 40s.The species, diet, and other environmental factors that humans can influence determine a turtle's lifespan.

The majority of turtles, if they live past their first few years of life, will most likely live for at least a few decades.

What Do Turtles Eat ?

The type of jaw a turtle has for masticating (chewing) food, where it lives, and the types of food sources that are available to it all affect what it eats. Sea turtles may consume

  • seagrasses,
  • algae,
  • sponges,
  • sea squirts,
  • shrimp
  • crab
  • jellyfish
  • cuttlefish
  • or sea cucumbers, depending on the species.

Green sea turles (Chelonia myadis), for instance, are herbivorous animals that consume algae and seagrasses.
